Laocoon: English

200. But now a vaster spectacle of fear
201. burst over us, to vex our startled souls.
202. Laocoon, that day by cast of lot
203. priest unto Neptune, was in act to slay
204. a huge bull at the god's appointed fane.
205. Lo! o'er the tranquil deep from Tenedos
206. appeared a pair (I shudder as I tell)
207. of vastly coiling serpents, side by side,
208. stretching along the waves, and to the shore
209. taking swift course; their necks were lifted high,
210. their gory dragon-crests o'ertopped the waves;
211. all else, half seen, trailed low along the sea;
212. while with loud cleavage of the foaming brine
213. their monstrous backs wound forward fold on fold.
214. Soon they made land; the furious bright eyes
215. glowed with ensanguined fire; their quivering tongues
216. lapped hungrily the hissing, gruesome jaws.
217. All terror-pale we fled. Unswerving then
218. the monsters to Laocoon made way.
219. First round the tender limbs of his two sons
220. each dragon coiled, and on the shrinking flesh
221. fixed fast and fed. Then seized they on the sire ,
222. who flew to aid, a javelin in his hand,
223. embracing close in bondage serpentine
224. twice round the waist; and twice in scaly grasp
225. around his neck, and o'er him grimly peered
226. with lifted head and crest; he, all the while,
227. his holy fillet fouled with venomous blood,
228. tore at his fetters with a desperate hand,
229. and lifted up such agonizing voice,
230. as when a bull, death-wounded, seeks to flee
231. the sacrificial altar, and thrusts back
232. from his doomed head the ill-aimed, glancing blade.
233. Then swiftly writhed the dragon-pair away
234. unto the templed height, and in the shrine
235. of cruel Pallas sure asylum found
236. beneath the goddess' feet and orbed shield.
237. Such trembling horror as we ne'er had known
238. seized now on every heart. “ Of his vast guilt
239. Laocoon,” they say, “ receives reward ;
240. for he with most abominable spear
241. did strike and violate that blessed wood.
242. Yon statue to the temple! Ask the grace
243. of glorious Pallas!” So the people cried
244. in general acclaim.
