Aeneas' Speech: English

192. Then back to shore he sped, and to his friends
193. distributed the spoil, with that rare wine
194. which good Acestes while in Sicily
195. had stored in jars, and prince-like sent away
196. with his Ioved guest;—this too Aeneas gave;
197. and with these words their mournful mood consoled.
198. “Companions mine, we have not failed to feel
199. calamity till now. O, ye have borne
200. far heavier sorrow: Jove will make an end
201. also of this. Ye sailed a course hard by
202. infuriate Scylla's howling cliffs and caves .
203. Ye knew the Cyclops' crags. Lift up your hearts!
204. No more complaint and fear! It well may be
205. some happier hour will find this memory fair.
206. Through chance and change and hazard without end,
207. our goal is Latium; where our destinies
208. beckon to blest abodes, and have ordained
209. that Troy shall rise new-born! Have patience all!
210. And bide expectantly that golden day.”
211. Such was his word, but vexed with grief and care,
212. feigned hopes upon his forehead firm he wore,
213. and locked within his heart a hero's pain.
